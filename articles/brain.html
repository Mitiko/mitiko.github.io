Brain

There're plenty of reasons to write a blog post about the brain - to better understand our thoughts, to inspire better AI architectures, to expand the field of psychology, and just out of pure curiosity. My intentions for this piece of writing is probably inspired by all but I'm mostly interested in extracting the core principles of the workings of the human brain and applying them to artificial systems. For that, we'd need to consider the differences between human and machine:

  • Processing (power and speed)
  • Memory (size and speed)
  • Sensors (resolution)
  • Faster processing
  • Better at (extremely) specific tasks - playing chess, forecasting weather, etc.
  • Better memory (exabytes of disk space and terabytes of random access memory)
  • Superior sensors (on-par or better than human senses, and improving)
  • Evolutionary approved design
  • More powerful in broader tasks - decision maing, content creation, problem solving, etc.
  • Adaptive reward function

Preface

I'd be lying if I said this is my brain theory, for it can barely be called a theory. I prefer you look at it as a proposition for a model of the brain, explored in the context of psychology and AI.

I'll be talking about the (functionally) different "parts" of the brain but be not mistaken, evolution is not so caring as to organize brain cells in tidy groups for us to understand them better.
